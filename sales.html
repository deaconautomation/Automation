LinkedInFirst Touch Message
Hey [First Name] — noticed you're running ops at [Company]. Quick question: how much time does your team spend each week moving data between tools manually? Asking because most ops leads I talk to are losing 15–20 hours a week to stuff that should be automated. Working on something that fixes that — happy to share if it's relevant.
Why it works: One question, zero pitch. Makes them answer and self-qualify. Don't add anything else.
LinkedInFollow-Up #1 — Day 4 (No Reply)
Hey [First Name] — just bumping this up. Totally understand if timing's off. One thing I'll leave you with: teams that automate their core workflows are moving 3× faster than those that don't. If that's a priority for [Company] this quarter, worth a 15-minute chat. Want me to send over a few examples of what we've automated for similar teams?
Tip: End with a yes/no question — it's easier to reply to than an open-ended ask.
LinkedInFollow-Up #2 — Day 9 (Break-Up)
[First Name] — last one from me, I promise. I built Vela specifically for ops leads who are too busy to deal with automation tools that require a developer. If that's ever you, the door's open. [Your Vela URL]
Why a break-up message works: People respond to finality. "Last one" often gets more replies than the first two combined.

CallCall Opener — First 60 Seconds
"Thanks for making the time, [First Name]. Before I show you anything, I want to make sure this is actually useful for you. Can you walk me through what a typical week looks like for your team in terms of repetitive work — what takes the most time that probably shouldn't?"
Let them talk for 3–4 minutes. Do not pitch yet. The more they talk, the more they sell themselves.
CallMirror Back Their Pain
"So it sounds like [their pain in their own words] is costing your team roughly [X hours] a week. Is that a problem you're actively trying to solve right now, or more of a background frustration?"
This question is critical. "Actively trying to solve" = hot lead. "Background frustration" = nurture. Treat them differently.

What I'm going to do is show you how Vela would handle [their specific pain] — I'll use your actual workflow so it's not hypothetical. If after 10 minutes it doesn't feel like a fit, just say so — no pressure at all. Sound good?"
CallClose / Next Step
"Based on everything you've told me — does this feel like something worth moving forward with? [If yes:] Great. The simplest next step is a 30-minute setup call where we actually build your first automation together. I can do [time] or [time] — which works better? [If not sure:] What would need to be true for this to be a yes?"
"What would need to be true" is the most powerful closing question in sales. It makes them tell you exactly what to address.

The rule with objections: never fight them. Acknowledge, then ask a question that makes them think. You want them to convince themselves.
💬 "We already use Zapier / Make"
"Good — Vela works alongside those. The difference is you describe what you want in plain English and it builds it. No node editors, no logic trees. Most teams come to us when Zapier gets too complex to manage. What does yours look like right now?"
💬 "We don't have budget right now"
"Totally fair. Out of curiosity — what's your team's rough hourly cost? I ask because if we're saving 15 hours a week across even two people, the ROI math usually makes the conversation worth having. Can I send you a quick breakdown?"
💬 "Just send me some info first"
"Of course. To make sure I send the right thing — what's the one workflow that would make the biggest difference if it ran automatically tomorrow?" That tells me exactly what to focus on.
💬 "We're too busy right now"
"That's exactly why most people come to us — being too busy is the symptom. When do you think would be a better time? And in the meantime — is the problem getting worse or staying the same?"
💬 "We have a developer who handles this"
"That's great — what's their backlog looking like? Most dev teams we work with are 6–8 weeks behind on internal tooling. Vela handles the repetitive stuff so your dev can focus on product. Worth a look?"
💬 "How is this different from [competitor]?"
"Good question. The main difference is how you build automations. With most tools you're dragging nodes and writing logic. With Vela you just describe what you want. Have you used [competitor] before? What did you find frustrating about it?"
💬 "I need to check with my team / boss"
"Of course — who else would be involved in the decision? I'm happy to put together something specific to your use case that makes it easy for them to say yes. What concerns do you think they'd have?"
💬 "We tried automation before and it didn't stick"
"That's really common — most automation tools require someone to maintain them constantly. What broke down? The reason most of our customers switched is because Vela self-heals and doesn't need babysitting."
🎯
The golden rule: Never open with what Vela is. Always open with what it solves — and make it specific to them. The best salespeople ask more questions than they answer.
RuleLead With Pain, Not Product
❌ "Hi, I have an AI automation tool called Vela that helps teams..." ✅ "How much time does your team spend each week on manual reporting?" Nobody cares about your product. They care about their problem. Make the first message entirely about them.
RuleSpecific Beats Generic Every Time
❌ "I help companies automate their workflows" ✅ "I help marketing agencies automate client reporting so they stop spending Fridays in spreadsheets" Specificity builds instant credibility. It signals you understand their world.
RuleOne Ask Per Message
❌ "Would love to connect, share more info, hop on a call, and show you a demo" ✅ "Worth a 15-minute call?" Every message should have exactly one call to action. More than one = decision fatigue = no reply.
RuleFollow Up 3× Before Moving On
80% of deals close after the 3rd–5th follow-up. Most people give up after one. Day 1 → First message Day 4 → Follow-up #1 (add value or new angle) Day 9 → Break-up message (creates urgency through finality) After that, move them to a "nurture later" list and circle back in 60 days.
RuleThe Best Closing Question in Sales
When someone is on the fence, ask: "What would need to be true for this to be a yes?" This forces them to articulate their real objection — and gives you exactly what to address. Most salespeople never ask it.
RuleSilence Is Your Best Tool on a Call
After you ask a question — stop talking. Count to 10 in your head if you have to. The person who speaks first after a question loses leverage. Let them fill the silence. They'll reveal their real concern, their budget, their urgency — everything you need to close.
